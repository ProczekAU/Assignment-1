IT Interest

What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start? Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience (if any).

IT has been an interest for me from a very young age. I would be given old broken computers and printers from family friends and I would tear them down. Not knowing what I was actually doing, I just enjoyed getting hands on with various devices. This eventually developed into actual attempts at fixing devices as I got into my teens, which in most cases was successful. Eventually I developed enough knowledge to start building custom computers, speakers, and doing all sorts of repairs for family and friends. Besides interest in hardware, I also developed an interest for front-end web development. One of my first jobs was for an online cigar business. Through skills I learnt with developing Tumblr blogs, I used these skills to assist in maintaining the website of the business. I then went to do my Diploma of Electrical and Communication Engineering, which led me into university. There I changed directions, and after two years realised that I need to return to my real passion, IT.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

I chose to come to RMIT for a couple key reasons. Firstly, I was not happy with the direction I had chosen at the University of Queensland. I wanted to change degrees and also move onto an online platform. The reason being that I did not like wasting my time and money having to travel into campus, and found that I could manage my own time better studying from home after finishing a Diploma in Web Design. The other reason is a little more political and simply put, I did not want to support UQ with what has been currently unfolding. I chose RMIT because I deemed it to be the best university offering this type of degree, purely online.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

The most important thing for me, is to find my niche. I have had very broad experience with many fields of IT, and seem to enjoy most, if not all of them. I want to narrow down my passion, and to find my dream, life-long career. If along the way I learn and develop other skills, then that is just icing on the cake.
